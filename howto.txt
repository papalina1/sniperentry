  Key answers to your final questions

  How discovery works: Birdeye /defi/v3/token/new-listing is polled every 15s. Each new mint gets a full /defi/token_overview call. Results are cached in-memory and continuously refreshed.

  How filtering works: 11 sequential hard rules (age, liquidity band, volume, buy count, buy/sell ratio, price change caps, price validity, suspicious patterns). First failure returns a plain-English reason
  immediately.

  How the score is calculated: Five positive factors (liquidity 0–20, momentum 0–20, buy pressure 0–20, timing 0–15, quote quality 0–10) minus five penalties (volatility, late entry, slippage, suspicious
  behaviour, concentration). Clamped to 0–100. Minimum to buy: 80.

  How the bot decides to buy: All 11 conditions must be true simultaneously — emergency stop off, daily loss under limit, position cap not reached, cooldown elapsed, no existing position in that token, token
  passed filters, score ≥ 80, Jupiter quote obtained, price impact acceptable.

  How the 2x target is calculated: targetPrice = entryPrice × 2.0. The bot also sells if price reaches 1.95× (buffer zone) to avoid missing the window due to execution latency.

  How the bot decides to sell (7 priorities):
  1. Emergency stop → immediate market sell
  2. Price ≥ 2.0× → take profit
  3. Price ≥ 1.95× → aggressive pre-target sell
  4. Price ≤ entry × 0.75 → stop loss (−25%)
  5. Time in trade > 20 minutes → timeout exit
  6. Liquidity dropped >30% from entry → emergency exit
  7. Sell pressure spike or momentum collapse → protective exit

  What to test in paper mode before live: Run 24–48 hours, confirm discovery logs tokens, filter reasons are sensible, scores are realistic, buys trigger on good setups, 2x target fires correctly, stop-loss
  fires at exactly −25%, timeout closes at 20 min, daily loss limit halts the bot, emergency stop closes all positions, and paper P&L is net positive over at least 10 trades.
